## What this skill takes from it

The two geometric OA indices (distal femoral W/L ratio; tibial IIOC height/width
ratio), the patella and peri-meniscal calcification volumes, the segmentation
scene, the landmark definitions, the scanner parameters, and the phenotype
cutoffs. Figures referenced in the resources (e.g. `assets/figure-4f-reference.png`
= Fig 4F) are crops from this paper. Supplemental Tables S1–S3 (per-specimen data)
inform the parameter choices; they are not vendored and are not a runtime input.

Out of scope from the paper: the tibial subchondral trabecular morphometry family
(Figure 1 / Table S1) — the paper measures it partly to show subchondral bone mass
is a contradictory OA metric, which is why the geometric indices exist.
